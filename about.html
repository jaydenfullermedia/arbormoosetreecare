Why "Moose"?

Mike and Sara Moes began Arbor Moose Tree Care in January 2019. Descending from Dutch heritage, our last name "Moes" is pronounced "Moose." Most of Mike's life he's been called Mr. Moose — so the name stuck.

Our hope when we started was simple: to make an honest living for our growing family. Something our children would grow up recognizing as hard work, dedication, sacrifice and humility. Seven years later, Mike, Sara and their four children continue to learn and grow as a family and as a business.

Then there's our right-hand man Will, in his third season and working through his arborist apprenticeship. A faithful man who's taken a liking to heights and a hard day's work. He greets our kids with kindness at the start and end of every day — we couldn't have asked for a better person to join the family business.

Good to know

What is an ISA Certified Arborist?

An ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) Certified Arborist is a tree care professional who has demonstrated a high level of knowledge by passing a comprehensive exam, adhering to a code of ethics, and committing to continued education.

Certified Arborists have a proven understanding of tree biology, identification, planting, pruning and other aspects of arboriculture. Certification is voluntary, not a government requirement — it's a commitment to the profession. To stay certified, arborists complete continuing education to keep up with the latest techniques and best practices.
